709維權律師大抓捕：法治的至暗時刻 709 Crackdown on Human Rights Lawyers: Darkest Hour for Rule of Law

2015年7月9日凌晨，北京著名人權律師王宇被警方從家中帶走。這標誌著一場針對中國維權律師和人權捍衛者的全國性大抓捕行動的開始。這場行動後來被稱為“709大抓捕”。

系統性的打壓

在隨後的幾天裡，全國23個省份的超過300名律師、律所人員、人權活動家被警方傳喚、刑拘、逮捕或失蹤。受影響的包括周世鋒、王全璋、李和平、謝陽等知名律師。

酷刑與認罪

許多被捕律師在被秘密關押期間遭受了嚴酷的身體和心理折磨，包括長時間剝奪睡眠、強制服藥、毆打等。當局還利用官方媒體播放被迫“認罪”的視頻，試圖抹黑律師群體。

法治的倒退

709大抓捕是對中國新興的維權律師群體的毀滅性打擊，也標誌著中國法治環境的急劇惡化。儘管如此，許多律師在獲釋後依然堅持信念，繼續為人權事業奮鬥。

In the early hours of July 9, 2015, prominent Beijing human rights lawyer Wang Yu was taken from her home by police. This marked the beginning of a nationwide crackdown on human rights lawyers and defenders in China, which later became known as the "709 Crackdown."

Systematic Suppression

In the days that followed, over 300 lawyers, law firm staff, and human rights activists across 23 provinces were summoned, criminally detained, arrested, or disappeared by police. Those affected included well-known lawyers such as Zhou Shifeng, Wang Quanzhang, Li Heping, and Xie Yang.

Torture and Confessions

Many arrested lawyers suffered severe physical and psychological torture during secret detention, including prolonged sleep deprivation, forced medication, and beatings. Authorities also used state media to broadcast forced "confessions" in an attempt to discredit the legal community.

Regression of Rule of Law

The 709 Crackdown was a devastating blow to China's emerging community of human rights lawyers and marked a sharp deterioration in the country's rule of law environment. Nevertheless, many lawyers have maintained their convictions after release and continue to fight for human rights.
