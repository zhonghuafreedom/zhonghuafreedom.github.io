劉霞：詩人、囚徒、亡夫之妻 Liu Xia: Poet, Prisoner, Widow of a Laureate

2010年10月8日，諾貝爾委員會宣布將和平獎授予獄中的劉曉波。當天起，他的妻子、詩人與攝影師劉霞被便衣警察封閉在北京的家中——沒有任何法律文書、沒有任何罪名，這場非法軟禁持續了將近八年。

沒有罪名的牢獄

軟禁期間，劉霞的住所大門外長期駐守警員與便衣，電話被切斷、互聯網被封鎖。她偶爾被允許探望獄中的劉曉波，但日常與外界的接觸幾近於零。她的弟弟劉暉也被牽連，於2013年被判刑11年。

詩與攝影中的窒息

劉霞早年是 1980 年代北京前衛詩歌的重要參與者，其攝影作品《醜娃娃》系列曾在歐美巡迴展出。她的詩寫道：

「我是一隻在桌上等了一輩子的杯子，注定要被碎裂。」

八年軟禁讓她罹患重度抑鬱與心臟病，西方友人一度擔憂她活不過下一個冬天。

2018年的離開

劉曉波於2017年7月病逝獄中。一年後的2018年7月10日，在德國總理梅克爾及多國長期斡旋下，劉霞獲准離開中國，飛抵柏林。下機時她已瘦得近乎不成人形，但低聲對前來迎接的友人說：「我自由了，但他不在。」

結語：被剝奪的自由，是國家的罪

劉霞從未被指控任何罪名，卻被剝奪了八年人生。她的存在揭穿了一個事實：在中國，做一個諾貝爾和平獎得主的妻子，本身就是一種「罪」。我們紀念她，也紀念所有「家屬連坐」的政治犯親屬。

On October 8, 2010, the Norwegian Nobel Committee awarded the Peace Prize to the imprisoned Liu Xiaobo. From that day on, his wife—poet and photographer Liu Xia—was sealed inside her Beijing home by plainclothes police, without any legal document or charge. This illegal house arrest would last nearly eight years.

A Prison Without Charges

Throughout her detention, officers were stationed outside her residence around the clock; her phone line was cut and internet access blocked. She was occasionally allowed to visit her husband in prison, but contact with the outside world was effectively zero. Her brother Liu Hui was also implicated and sentenced to 11 years in 2013.

Suffocation in Poetry and Photography

Liu Xia had been a key figure in Beijing's avant-garde poetry of the 1980s, and her "Ugly Baby" photographic series toured Europe and the US. She once wrote:

"I am a cup that has waited my whole life on the table, destined to be shattered."

Eight years of confinement left her with severe depression and heart disease; friends in the West feared she might not survive another winter.

Departure in 2018

Liu Xiaobo died in custody in July 2017. A year later, on July 10, 2018, after sustained pressure from German Chancellor Angela Merkel and other governments, Liu Xia was permitted to leave China and flew to Berlin. She emerged from the plane gaunt and almost unrecognizable, whispering to waiting friends: "I am free, but he is not."

Conclusion: Freedom Stolen Is a Crime of the State

Liu Xia was never charged with anything, yet eight years of her life were taken from her. Her case exposes a chilling truth: in China, being the wife of a Nobel laureate is itself a "crime." We remember her—and every family member punished by association.
