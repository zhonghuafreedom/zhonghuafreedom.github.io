香港 47 人案：戰後最大規模政治審判 The Hong Kong 47: The Largest Political Trial Since World War II

2021 年 1 月 6 日清晨，香港警方以「違反國安法・串謀顛覆國家政權罪」拘捕 53 名曾參與 2020 年立法會初選的民主派人士。最終 47 人被起訴，從學者戴耀廷到 23 歲的岑敖暉，從前立法會議員到工會領袖，幾乎涵蓋一整代民主派菁英。這場審判被稱為「香港 47 人案」，成為香港主權移交以來最大規模的政治起訴。

背景：35+ 民主派初選

2020 年 7 月，民主派為了在 2020 立法會選舉中爭取「35+」過半議席，由戴耀廷與區諾軒等人發起初選，逾 60 萬港人投票。北京旋即定性為「顛覆陰謀」，國安法生效僅兩週，《香港眾志》宣布解散。立法會選舉隨後被押後一年，並修改選舉制度確保「愛國者治港」。

長期還押：未審先囚

除少數人獲保釋，多數被告自 2021 年 3 月被還押，至 2024 年判刑時已被關押超過 1,200 天，遠超被告若獲較輕刑期可能的服刑時間。律師多次申請保釋遭拒，理由是「國安案件原則上不予保釋」。

2024 年 5 月裁定有罪

2024年5月30日，西九龍裁判法院（升格為高等法院原訟法庭）裁定 14 名不認罪被告中 14 人罪成、2 人脫罪。法庭認定民主派「以否決財政預算案逼迫政府回應五大訴求」屬「顛覆」。判決遭到國際法律界與聯合國人權專家強烈批評。

判刑：戴耀廷 10 年

2024 年 11 月 19 日，主審法官對 45 名被告進行判刑。發起初選的法律學者戴耀廷被判 10 年——47 人案中最重判刑。前立法會議員區諾軒、趙家賢分別獲 6 年與 7 年。其餘多人介於 4 至 7 年。

結語：選舉並非顛覆

香港 47 人案的真正震撼，並不在於量刑，而在於——他們的「罪」其實是參與一場合法的初選。當「投票」被定性為「顛覆」，當「否決預算案」被視為「武力或威脅」，香港的法治與民主便在同一日被宣判死亡。

In the early hours of January 6, 2021, Hong Kong police arrested 53 pro-democracy figures who had participated in the July 2020 legislative primary, charging them with "conspiracy to commit subversion" under the National Security Law. Forty-seven were ultimately prosecuted, ranging from scholar Benny Tai to 23-year-old Lester Shum, from former legislators to union leaders— spanning almost an entire generation of democratic elites. The trial, known as the "Hong Kong 47," became the largest political prosecution since the handover.

Background: The "35+" Pro-Democracy Primary

In July 2020, hoping to win a majority ("35+") in the upcoming Legislative Council election, democrats led by Benny Tai and Au Nok-hin held an unofficial primary in which over 600,000 Hongkongers voted. Beijing immediately branded it a "subversive plot." Just two weeks after the NSL took effect, the activist group Demosisto disbanded, the LegCo election was postponed by a year, and the electoral system was overhauled to ensure "patriots governing Hong Kong."

Pre-Trial Detention: Imprisoned Before Convicted

Except for a handful released on bail, most defendants were remanded from March 2021. By the time of sentencing in 2024 they had been detained over 1,200 days—often exceeding the time they might have served under shorter sentences. Multiple bail applications were denied on the rationale that "NSL cases as a rule do not allow bail."

May 2024 Verdict

On May 30, 2024, the West Kowloon Magistrates' Courts (sitting as the Court of First Instance) convicted 14 of 16 defendants who had pleaded not guilty. The court accepted the prosecution's theory that the democrats' plan to "veto the budget to force government concessions on the Five Demands" amounted to subversion. International legal observers and UN human rights experts strongly criticized the verdict.

Sentencing: Benny Tai Receives 10 Years

On November 19, 2024, sentences were handed down for 45 defendants. Benny Tai, the legal scholar who initiated the primary, received 10 years—the heaviest sentence. Former legislators Au Nok-hin and Andrew Chiu received 6 and 7 years respectively. Most others received between 4 and 7 years.

Conclusion: Elections Are Not Subversion

The true shock of the Hong Kong 47 case lies not in the lengths of the sentences but in the nature of the "crime"—participation in a lawful primary election. When voting becomes "subversion" and budget vetoes become "force or threat," the rule of law and democracy in Hong Kong were pronounced dead on the same day.
