王炳章：被綁架的中國民運先驅 Wang Bingzhang: The Kidnapped Founder of China's Overseas Democracy Movement

王炳章被譽為「海外中國民運之父」。1982年，他放棄即將完成的醫學博士前途，在紐約創辦《中國之春》雜誌與「中國民主團結聯盟」，是 1980 年代海外最具影響力的中國異議組織者之一。2002年，他在越南被中共特工綁架回中國，從此身陷囹圄至今。

從醫生到革命者

王炳章 1979 年公派加拿大麥吉爾大學攻讀心臟病學博士，本可在北美擁有富裕生活，但他在 1982 年發表《告別醫學生涯》宣言，全身投入海外民運。他主張和平、理性、非暴力的政體轉型，同時也提出「中華聯邦」構想。

越南邊境的綁架

2002年6月26日，王炳章與兩名同伴前往越南會見內地民運人士。他們在芒街遭一群操中國方言的「綁匪」綁架，越過中越邊境送回廣東。中國當局聲稱這是「越方移交」，但所有跡象顯示這是中共國安部跨境執法的典型案例。

不公開的審判與無期徒刑

2003年2月10日，深圳市中級人民法院對王炳章進行不公開審判，以「組織、領導恐怖組織罪」、「為境外刺探、刺取國家秘密罪」判處無期徒刑——這是改革開放後首位被判無期的海外民運人士。國際特赦組織及多國政府均拒絕接受其定罪。

長期單獨監禁與健康惡化

王炳章於廣東韶關北江監獄被長期單獨關押，至 2025 年已超過 22 年。家屬探視被嚴格限制，他多次中風與心血管疾病發作。其姊王玉華、女兒王天安在海外為其奔走多年，呼籲國際社會關注。

結語：他選擇了最艱難的那條路

當大多數海外華人選擇沉默或回國效忠時，王炳章選擇了最艱難的一條路——在自由世界裡為不自由的故鄉發聲。他的人生提醒我們：真正的勇敢，是明知歸途有囚籠，仍堅持踏上歸途。

Wang Bingzhang is widely regarded as the "father of China's overseas democracy movement." In 1982, he abandoned a near-completed medical doctorate to found Beijing Spring magazine and the Chinese Alliance for Democracy in New York—becoming one of the most influential overseas Chinese dissident organizers of the 1980s. In 2002, he was kidnapped from Vietnam by Chinese agents and has been imprisoned in China ever since.

From Doctor to Revolutionary

Sent abroad in 1979 to study cardiology at McGill University in Canada, Wang could have enjoyed a prosperous life in North America. Instead, in 1982 he published a "Farewell to Medicine" declaration and devoted himself entirely to the overseas democracy cause. He advocated peaceful, rational, non-violent regime transition and proposed a "Federation of China."

Kidnapped at the Vietnamese Border

On June 26, 2002, Wang travelled to Vietnam with two companions to meet mainland activists. They were seized in Móng Cái by abductors speaking Chinese dialects and smuggled back into Guangdong. Chinese authorities claimed it was a "Vietnamese handover," but all evidence points to a classic case of cross-border enforcement by China's Ministry of State Security.

Closed-Door Trial and Life Sentence

On February 10, 2003, the Shenzhen Intermediate People's Court tried Wang in closed proceedings and sentenced him to life imprisonment for "organizing and leading a terrorist organization" and "spying for foreign powers." He became the first overseas democracy activist to receive a life sentence in the Reform Era. Amnesty International and several governments refused to accept the conviction.

Long-Term Solitary Confinement and Failing Health

Wang has been held in long-term solitary confinement at Beijiang Prison in Shaoguan, Guangdong, for over 22 years as of 2025. Family visits are severely restricted; he has suffered multiple strokes and cardiovascular crises. His sister Wang Yuhua and daughter Wang Tian'an have campaigned tirelessly overseas for international attention.

Conclusion: He Chose the Hardest Road

When most overseas Chinese chose silence or returned to pledge loyalty, Wang Bingzhang chose the hardest road—speaking out, from freedom, for an unfree homeland. His life reminds us: true courage is knowing the road home leads to a cell, and walking it anyway.
