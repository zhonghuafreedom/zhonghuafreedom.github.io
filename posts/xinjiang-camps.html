新疆再教育營：21世紀的種族滅絕 Xinjiang Re-education Camps: A 21st-Century Genocide

2017年起，中共在新疆維吾爾自治區大規模建設「職業技能教育培訓中心」——一個被掩蓋在發展話語下的監獄體系。多份洩漏文件、衛星圖像與倖存者證詞顯示：截至2019年，至少有100萬至180萬維吾爾人、哈薩克人及其他突厥民族穆斯林被強制關押。

「中國電文」與「新疆警察檔案」

2019年國際調查記者同盟（ICIJ）公布「中國電文」，2022年「新疆警察檔案」進一步曝光逾2000張被關押者照片，包括15歲少女與73歲老人。文件詳述「逃跑即射殺」的內部命令與營區管理細則，徹底擊碎北京「自願培訓」的謊言。

強制勞動與全球供應鏈

大量被釋放者被強制送往內地工廠或新疆兵團棉田工作。澳大利亞戰略政策研究所（ASPI）2020年報告顯示，至少82個全球品牌——包括Apple、Nike、BMW、Volkswagen等——的供應鏈疑似涉及維族強制勞動。美國於2022年通過《維吾爾強迫勞動預防法》，要求進口商證明非強制勞動所產。

強制絕育與文化清洗

學者鄭國恩（Adrian Zenz）2020年研究指出，2015至2018年，新疆維族婦女出生率下降近50%。當局對育齡女性大規模強制裝環、結紮、墮胎。同時，超過1萬6千座清真寺被拆毀或改造，維吾爾語教學遭限制，傳統文化遭系統性清洗。

國際定性與北京否認

美國、英國、加拿大、荷蘭、立陶宛、捷克等國政府或國會已將新疆所發生的事認定為「種族滅絕」。2022年聯合國人權事務高級專員辦事處發布報告，認為「可能構成國際法下的反人類罪」。北京則持續以「反恐」、「去極端化」為由全盤否認。

家庭分離與兒童同化

集中營之外，另一套同化制度正在摧毀家庭。大量父母被拘押、服刑或送往工廠後，兒童被送入寄宿學校與國家福利機構，接受漢語、愛國主義與去宗教化教育。當一代孩子與母語、信仰和家庭記憶被切斷，文化滅絕就不只發生在營牆之內。

結語：靜默的營，喧囂的世界

當你閱讀這篇文章時，仍有數十萬名維吾爾人被分隔於圍牆與監視塔之中。他們沒有名字、沒有判決、沒有刑期。我們紀錄這一切，是因為人類的記憶不應被效率掩蓋，文明的底線不應被沉默取代。

Beginning in 2017, the Chinese Communist Party launched a massive build-out of "Vocational Skills Education and Training Centers" across the Xinjiang Uyghur Autonomous Region—a prison system disguised in the language of development. Leaked documents, satellite imagery, and survivor testimonies reveal that by 2019, between 1 and 1.8 million Uyghurs, Kazakhs, and other Turkic Muslims had been forcibly detained.

The China Cables and the Xinjiang Police Files

In 2019, the International Consortium of Investigative Journalists (ICIJ) released the "China Cables." In 2022, the "Xinjiang Police Files" exposed over 2,000 photographs of detainees, including a 15-year-old girl and a 73-year-old elder. The documents detailed internal "shoot to kill on escape" orders and operational manuals, shattering Beijing's claim of "voluntary training."

Forced Labor and the Global Supply Chain

Many released detainees were transferred to mainland factories or sent to cotton fields under the Xinjiang Production and Construction Corps. A 2020 Australian Strategic Policy Institute (ASPI) report identified at least 82 global brands—including Apple, Nike, BMW, and Volkswagen—whose supply chains were suspected of involving Uyghur forced labor. The United States enacted the Uyghur Forced Labor Prevention Act in 2022, requiring importers to prove their goods were not produced with forced labor.

Forced Sterilization and Cultural Erasure

Research by scholar Adrian Zenz in 2020 documented a nearly 50% drop in Uyghur birth rates between 2015 and 2018. Authorities have carried out mass forced IUD insertions, sterilizations, and abortions on women of childbearing age. Meanwhile, over 16,000 mosques have been destroyed or altered, Uyghur-language education restricted, and traditional culture systematically erased.

International Designation, Beijing's Denial

The United States, United Kingdom, Canada, the Netherlands, Lithuania, the Czech Republic, and other governments or parliaments have formally designated the situation in Xinjiang as "genocide." A 2022 report by the UN High Commissioner for Human Rights concluded the abuses "may constitute international crimes, in particular crimes against humanity." Beijing continues to deny everything in the name of "counter-terrorism" and "de-radicalization."

Family Separation and Child Assimilation

Beyond the camps, another system of assimilation is destroying families. As parents are detained, imprisoned, or transferred to factories, children are sent to boarding schools and state welfare institutions for Mandarin, patriotic, and de-religionized education. When a generation is cut off from mother tongue, faith, and family memory, cultural erasure occurs not only inside camp walls.

Conclusion: Silent Camps in a Noisy World

As you read this, hundreds of thousands of Uyghurs remain behind walls and watchtowers. They have no names, no verdicts, no fixed sentences. We document this because human memory must not be overwritten by convenience, and the moral floor of civilization must not be ceded to silence.
