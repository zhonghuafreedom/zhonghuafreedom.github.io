艾未未：藝術作為抵抗 Ai Weiwei: Art as Resistance

艾未未——前衛藝術家、紀錄片導演、社會活動家，是當代中國少數能將美學與抵抗融為一體的人物。從汶川地震遇難學生姓名牆，到秘密拘留 81 天，再到流亡歐洲，他的人生本身就是一件持續的政治作品。

汶川公民調查

2008年5月汶川大地震，數千名學生因「豆腐渣校舍」倒塌而死，當局拒絕公開名單。艾未未發起公民調查，召集志願者逐戶走訪，最終整理出 5,196 位罹難學生的完整名單，並在德國慕尼黑藝術之家用 9,000 個書包拼出殉難母親的話：「她在這個世界上開心地生活了七年。」

2011 年的 81 天

2011 年 4 月 3 日，艾未未在北京機場準備飛往香港時被警方帶走，隨後失蹤 81 天。家屬與律師完全不知其下落。獲釋後當局以「逃稅」名目向其工作室追討 1,500 萬人民幣巨款。數萬名中國網民匿名捐款支持，這一民間集資被視為對「以稅治人」式政治打壓的公開反抗。

從監視到流亡

艾未未的工作室與住所被裝設多達 15 個監視攝像頭，他公開「直播」自我監控以諷刺。2015 年護照被歸還後，他離開中國前往德國、英國、葡萄牙等地，至今未能返國。他的紀錄片《人流》（Human Flow，2017）追蹤全球難民危機，將個人經歷昇華為對所有失去家園者的同理。

結語：藝術的政治性

艾未未常說：「藝術如果不為人權發聲，那不過是裝飾。」他的瓷瓜子、自行車森林、汶川名單，都不是傳統意義上的「美」，而是直視傷口的勇氣。他證明了：在一個禁止記憶的國度，記住，本身就是反抗。

Ai Weiwei—avant-garde artist, documentarian, and activist—is one of the few contemporary Chinese figures who has fused aesthetics with resistance. From the wall of names for students killed in the Sichuan earthquake to his 81-day secret detention and eventual exile, his life is itself an ongoing political work.

The Sichuan Citizen Investigation

In the May 2008 Sichuan earthquake, thousands of students died when shoddily built schools collapsed. Authorities refused to release names. Ai launched a citizen investigation, recruiting volunteers who went door to door and ultimately compiled a list of 5,196 deceased students. At the Haus der Kunst in Munich, he arranged 9,000 backpacks to spell out the words of one bereaved mother: "She lived happily for seven years in this world."

The 81 Days of 2011

On April 3, 2011, Ai was seized at Beijing Capital Airport on his way to Hong Kong. He disappeared for 81 days, with neither family nor lawyers informed of his whereabouts. After his release, authorities pursued him for 15 million yuan in alleged "back taxes." Tens of thousands of Chinese netizens anonymously donated to support him—a rare public act of defiance against politically motivated tax persecution.

From Surveillance to Exile

Up to 15 surveillance cameras were installed around Ai's home and studio. He responded by self-streaming his own monitoring, mocking the practice. After his passport was returned in 2015, he left China for Germany, the UK, and Portugal, never to return. His 2017 film "Human Flow" follows the global refugee crisis, transmuting personal experience into empathy for all who have lost their homes.

Conclusion: The Politics of Art

Ai often says, "Art that does not speak for human rights is mere decoration." His porcelain sunflower seeds, forests of bicycles, and Sichuan name lists are not "beautiful" in the conventional sense—they are the courage to look at a wound. He proves that in a country that forbids memory, to remember is itself an act of resistance.
