王全璋律師：709事件中失蹤的良知 Lawyer Wang Quanzhang: A Conscience Disappeared in the 709 Crackdown

2015年7月10日，709大抓捕第二天，維權律師王全璋在北京被警方帶走。他長期代理法輪功學員、被拆遷者與良心犯案件，是當局眼中最棘手的人權律師之一。從那一刻起，他從中國的公共視野中徹底消失，整整 1,200 多天。

三年半的「指定居所監視居住」

當局以「煽動顛覆國家政權罪」對其指定居所監視居住，這種制度允許警方將嫌疑人秘密關押於不明地點長達半年，無需通知家屬。王全璋被切斷與所有律師、家人的聯繫，妻子李文足多次詢問下落均被拒絕。

李文足的「千里尋夫」

李文足為尋找丈夫，曾從北京徒步走向天津的看守所，剃光頭以示抗爭，並與其他709律師家屬組成「709妻子團」。她們披白衣、戴口罩，在中國少有的女性公民抗議中以柔軟卻堅定的姿態長期站立街頭，成為國際社會了解709案的關鍵聲音。

秘密審判與重刑

2018年12月26日，天津市第二中級人民法院在不允許家屬與獨立律師到場的情況下，秘密開庭審理王全璋案。2019年1月28日宣判：以「顛覆國家政權罪」判處王全璋有期徒刑四年六個月。

釋放後仍被監視

2020年4月王全璋刑滿釋放，但被以「防疫隔離」之名繼續隔離於濟南，此後輾轉多地，妻兒被監視，家屬租房屢遭驅趕。即使「自由」，他仍未真正離開那個看不見的牢籠。

結語：律師代表的不只是當事人

王全璋的「罪」，是他願意走進那些沒有人願意接的案子。當一個國家連為弱者辯護的律師都要被消失，這個國家的法律便已不存在。我們紀念王全璋與所有 709 律師——他們守護的不只是當事人，而是法律本身的尊嚴。

On July 10, 2015—the second day of the 709 Crackdown—human rights lawyer Wang Quanzhang was seized by police in Beijing. Long known for defending Falun Gong practitioners, evicted homeowners, and prisoners of conscience, he was among the most thorn-in-side lawyers from the authorities' perspective. From that moment, he vanished from public view for over 1,200 days.

Three and a Half Years of "Residential Surveillance"

Authorities placed him under "Residential Surveillance at a Designated Location" on suspicion of "inciting subversion of state power"—a system allowing police to hold suspects in undisclosed locations for up to six months without informing families. Wang was cut off from all lawyers and relatives. His wife Li Wenzu was repeatedly denied any information about his whereabouts.

Li Wenzu's "Thousand-Mile March for Her Husband"

To find her husband, Li set out on foot from Beijing toward a detention center in Tianjin and publicly shaved her head in protest. Together with other wives of the 709 lawyers, she formed the "709 Wives." Dressed in white and wearing face masks, they engaged in one of the rare sustained female-led civic protests in China and became a key voice informing the world about the 709 case.

Secret Trial and Heavy Sentence

On December 26, 2018, the Tianjin No. 2 Intermediate People's Court tried Wang in secret, barring family and independent lawyers from attendance. On January 28, 2019, he was sentenced to four years and six months in prison for "subversion of state power."

Surveillance After Release

Wang was formally released in April 2020 but immediately placed under "epidemic isolation" in Jinan, then forced to relocate repeatedly. His wife and child remain under surveillance, and landlords are pressured to evict them. Even "free," he has yet to leave the invisible cage.

Conclusion: A Lawyer Represents More Than a Client

Wang Quanzhang's "crime" was his willingness to take cases no one else would. When the lawyers who defend the powerless are themselves disappeared, the law itself ceases to exist. We remember Wang and all the 709 lawyers—they did not only defend their clients, but the very dignity of the law.
