四通橋勇士彭載舟：孤身抗議獨裁 Peng Zaizhou: The Lone Protester at Sitong Bridge

2022年10月13日，在中共二十大即將召開、北京安保森嚴的時刻，一名男子在北京海淀區四通橋上掛起了兩條白底紅字的巨型橫幅，並點燃狼煙，通過擴音器高呼口號。

震撼世界的標語

橫幅上寫著：“不要核酸要吃飯，不要封控要自由，不要謊言要尊嚴，不要文革要改革，不要領袖要選票，不做奴才做公民。” 另一條橫幅則直接點名：“罷課罷工罷免獨裁國賊習近平”。

彭載舟是誰？

這位抗議者隨後被確認為彭立發（網名彭載舟），一名物理學愛好者和科技工作者。他在行動前已經在網上發布了詳細的《致全國同胞書》，呼籲進行和平的非暴力不合作運動。

白紙運動前的火種

四通橋抗議發生在中共二十大前夕，當時北京進入高度戒備，網絡平台對「四通橋」「勇士」「橋」等詞迅速封禁。正因如此，彭載舟的行動顯得格外震撼：在一個連轉發都可能被抓的國家，他選擇用最公開的方式，把人民心中的恐懼說出來。

失蹤與連坐

事件後，彭載舟本人被迅速帶走，至今缺乏透明審判與可靠消息。網上轉發照片、悼念、製作標語的普通人也被約談、拘留或封號。這種連坐式清算，正說明政權真正恐懼的不是一條橫幅，而是人們發現自己並不孤單。

深遠的影響

彭載舟當場被捕，至今下落不明。但他的口號在隨後的“白紙運動”中被廣泛傳頌，成為了反抗極權的標誌性語言。他的勇氣證明了，即使在最黑暗的時刻，良知的火種依然未滅。

On October 13, 2022, just before the 20th Party Congress and amidst tight security in Beijing, a man hung two giant white banners with red characters on Sitong Bridge in Haidian District. He lit smoke signals and chanted slogans through a loudspeaker.

Slogans That Shook the World

The banners read: "We want food, not PCR tests. We want freedom, not lockdowns. We want dignity, not lies. We want reform, not a Cultural Revolution. We want votes, not a leader. We want to be citizens, not slaves." Another banner directly named the target: "Strike at schools and work, remove the dictatorial traitor Xi Jinping."

Who is Peng Zaizhou?

The protester was later identified as Peng Lifa (online alias Peng Zaizhou), a physics enthusiast and technology worker. Before his action, he had published a detailed "Letter to Compatriots Nationwide" online, calling for a peaceful non-violent non-cooperation movement.

The Spark Before the White Paper Movement

The Sitong Bridge protest occurred on the eve of the CCP's 20th Party Congress, when Beijing was under intense security control and online platforms instantly censored words such as "Sitong Bridge," "warrior," and even "bridge." That is why Peng's act was so powerful: in a country where even reposting can lead to arrest, he chose the most public way to speak the fear and anger many people kept private.

Disappearance and Collective Punishment

After the incident, Peng was swiftly taken away, and no transparent trial or reliable public information has emerged. Ordinary people who shared photos, mourned him, or reproduced the slogans were questioned, detained, or banned online. This collective punishment shows that the regime feared not one banner, but the possibility that people would discover they were not alone.

Profound Impact

Peng Zaizhou was arrested on the spot, and his whereabouts remain unknown. However, his slogans were widely chanted during the subsequent "White Paper Movement," becoming iconic language of resistance against totalitarianism. His courage proved that even in the darkest moments, the spark of conscience remains unextinguished.
