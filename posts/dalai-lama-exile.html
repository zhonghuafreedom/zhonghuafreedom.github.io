達賴喇嘛流亡與西藏抗暴 The Dalai Lama in Exile and the Tibetan Uprising

1959年3月10日，數萬西藏人聚集在拉薩布達拉宮外，抗議中共軍隊以「邀請觀看演出」為名意圖綁架達賴喇嘛。短短數日，這場「拉薩抗暴」演變為對中國駐軍的全面起義，遭解放軍血腥鎮壓，數萬藏人罹難。3月17日深夜，年僅24歲的第十四世達賴喇嘛被迫翻越喜馬拉雅山，流亡印度。

1950年的「和平解放」

1950年，解放軍以「和平解放西藏」之名進入昌都。1951年，西藏代表在北京被迫簽署《十七條協議》。隨後的「民主改革」摧毀寺院、剝奪世俗與宗教菁英財產，引爆累積至1959年的全面抵抗。

達蘭薩拉與藏人行政中央

達賴喇嘛抵達印度後，於達蘭薩拉建立流亡政府——「藏人行政中央」。逾15萬藏人陸續隨之流亡，建立寺院、學校、醫院。達賴喇嘛於2011年主動退出政治權力，將行政事務交予民選的「司政」（Sikyong），開啟藏人民主化先例。

1989年戒嚴與2008年再起義

1989年3月，拉薩再次爆發大規模抗議，時任西藏黨委書記的胡錦濤宣布戒嚴並血腥鎮壓。2008年北京奧運前夕，西藏全境再次爆發抗議，隨後逾150名藏人以自焚方式抗議高壓統治——這是世界宗教史上罕見的群體性自焚潮。

文化滅絕的進行式

近年來，當局在西藏推行「寄宿制學校」，據聯合國人權專家估計約有100萬藏族兒童被迫離開家庭、進入以漢語授課的封閉學校。寺院的活佛轉世程序須經北京審批，傳統節日被「中國化」，西藏自治區的數位監控密度甚至超過新疆。

結語：山可以阻隔身體，阻隔不了信念

達賴喇嘛已年邁，但他用六十多年告訴世人：暴力可以驅離一個人，卻無法消滅一種文明。當每一座被毀的寺院仍有一位倖存的喇嘛在朗誦經文，當每一個流亡藏童仍學著用藏文寫下自己的名字，西藏就還在。

On March 10, 1959, tens of thousands of Tibetans gathered outside the Potala Palace in Lhasa to protest a suspected plot by the Chinese military to abduct the Dalai Lama under the pretext of inviting him to a performance. Within days, the "Lhasa Uprising" evolved into a full revolt against Chinese occupation. The People's Liberation Army responded with brutal force, leaving tens of thousands of Tibetans dead. Late on March 17, the 14th Dalai Lama, then only 24, fled over the Himalayas into exile in India.

The 1950 "Peaceful Liberation"

In 1950, the PLA entered Chamdo under the banner of "peacefully liberating Tibet." In 1951, Tibetan representatives in Beijing were forced to sign the Seventeen Point Agreement. The subsequent "Democratic Reforms" destroyed monasteries and stripped religious and secular elites of their property, fueling the resistance that exploded in 1959.

Dharamsala and the Central Tibetan Administration

After arriving in India, the Dalai Lama established a government-in-exile—the Central Tibetan Administration—in Dharamsala. More than 150,000 Tibetans followed him into exile, building monasteries, schools, and hospitals. In 2011, the Dalai Lama voluntarily relinquished political authority, transferring it to the democratically elected "Sikyong"—an unprecedented act of democratization for the Tibetan community.

1989 Martial Law and the 2008 Uprising

In March 1989, large-scale protests erupted again in Lhasa. Then-Tibet Party Secretary Hu Jintao declared martial law and ordered a bloody crackdown. On the eve of the 2008 Beijing Olympics, protests swept across Tibet. Subsequently, more than 150 Tibetans set themselves on fire in protest against repressive rule—a wave of self-immolation almost without parallel in modern religious history.

Cultural Erasure in Progress

In recent years, authorities have implemented "boarding schools" in Tibet. UN human rights experts estimate that nearly one million Tibetan children have been forcibly separated from their families and placed in closed schools where instruction is in Mandarin. The recognition of reincarnated lamas now requires Beijing's approval, traditional festivals are being "Sinicized," and digital surveillance density in the Tibet Autonomous Region reportedly exceeds that of Xinjiang.

Conclusion: Mountains Can Block Bodies, Not Beliefs

The Dalai Lama has aged, but for more than six decades he has shown the world that violence may drive a person from their homeland but cannot erase a civilization. So long as one surviving monk still chants in every destroyed monastery, and one exiled Tibetan child still writes their name in Tibetan script, Tibet endures.
