2024年度中國人權狀況報告 2024 Annual Report on Human Rights in China

本報告詳細記錄了過去一年中，中國在言論自由、網絡審查以及少數民族權益方面的倒退。儘管國際社會持續關注，但中共當局對異見人士的打壓力度依然不減。

言論自由的寒冬

2024年，中國當局進一步收緊了對言論自由的控制。數十名獨立記者、博主和社會活動家因在社交媒體上發表批評政府的言論而被捕。其中，因報導社會不公而被控“尋釁滋事”的案例顯著增加。

網絡審查的升級

隨著人工智能技術的發展，中國的網絡審查系統變得更加智能化和無孔不入。關鍵詞過濾、圖像識別和語義分析技術被廣泛應用於監控網絡輿論。任何涉及政治敏感話題的討論都會在幾分鐘內被刪除。

少數民族權益的持續惡化

在新疆和西藏，針對少數民族的同化政策仍在繼續。報告指出，強制勞動、宗教限制和文化清洗的現象依然普遍存在。維吾爾族和藏族兒童被強制送入寄宿學校，接受漢化教育。

香港：國安法下的沈默

香港的公民社會空間被進一步壓縮。根據國安法，更多的民主派人士被判刑，獨立媒體被迫關閉。曾經充滿活力的香港，如今已難以聽到反對的聲音。

This report details the regression in freedom of speech, internet censorship, and minority rights in China over the past year. Despite continued international attention, the CCP authorities' crackdown on dissidents remains unabated.

Winter of Free Speech

In 2024, Chinese authorities further tightened control over freedom of speech. Dozens of independent journalists, bloggers, and activists were arrested for posting comments critical of the government on social media. Notably, cases of individuals charged with "picking quarrels and provoking trouble" for reporting on social injustices increased significantly.

Escalation of Internet Censorship

With the development of artificial intelligence technology, China's internet censorship system has become more intelligent and pervasive. Keyword filtering, image recognition, and semantic analysis technologies are widely used to monitor online public opinion. Any discussion involving politically sensitive topics is deleted within minutes.

Deterioration of Minority Rights

In Xinjiang and Tibet, assimilation policies targeting ethnic minorities continue. The report notes that forced labor, religious restrictions, and cultural cleansing remain prevalent. Uyghur and Tibetan children are forcibly sent to boarding schools to receive Sinicized education.

Hong Kong: Silence Under the National Security Law

Civil society space in Hong Kong has been further compressed. Under the National Security Law, more pro-democracy figures have been sentenced, and independent media outlets have been forced to close. Hong Kong, once vibrant, is now devoid of opposition voices.
