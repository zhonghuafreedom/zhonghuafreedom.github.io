江天勇律師：被酷刑與直播認罪的維權者 Lawyer Jiang Tianyong: Tortured and Forced into a Televised Confession

江天勇是 2000 年代以來中國最活躍的維權律師之一，曾代理法輪功學員、藏人、訪民、愛滋感染者、地下教會等大批最敏感案件。709 大抓捕後，他仍堅持為被捕同行家屬奔走，最終在 2016 年底被秘密拘押。其案件揭示了中國「電視認罪秀」與制度性酷刑的真實樣貌。

從河南鄉村教師到維權律師

江天勇 1971 年生於河南，曾是中學歷史教師。2003年取得律師資格後迅速投身公益訴訟，與許志永、滕彪等人共同發起「公盟」，是中國第一代維權律師群體的核心人物。他多次被吊銷執照、毆打、短期拘留。

為 709 律師家屬奔走後失蹤

2015 年 7 月以後，江天勇成為 709 案律師家屬的主要法律顧問之一，協助王全璋妻子李文足、謝陽妻子陳桂秋等發聲。2016 年 11 月 21 日，他在前往湖南長沙會見謝陽妻子後失聯，當局起初否認對其採取任何措施，直至 36 天後才正式承認羈押。

「指定居所監視居住」與酷刑指控

江天勇在指定居所監視居住期間，被剝奪睡眠、被強制服用不明藥物、被長時間定位審訊。2017 年 5 月在湖南長沙的庭審中，他在央視鏡頭前公開「認罪」，否認此前一切維權陳述。其妻金變玲指出，這完全違背他被捕前的人格與信念。多名律師懷疑其在拘押期間遭受嚴重精神與藥物折磨。

判刑與「軟釋放」

2017年11月21日，長沙市中級人民法院以「煽動顛覆國家政權罪」判處江天勇有期徒刑兩年。2019 年 2 月 28 日刑滿後，他被送回河南老家，至今仍處於變相軟禁，行動受嚴密監控，無法重新執業。

電視認罪的製造流程

江天勇案顯示，電視認罪往往不是司法程序，而是一套政治傳播工程：先以秘密羈押切斷律師與家屬，再以酷刑、藥物或心理壓迫摧毀意志，最後由官方媒體剪輯成「自願悔罪」的畫面。這種畫面不是證據，而是恐嚇其他法律人的工具。

結語：認罪並非真相

江天勇案揭穿了一個事實：中國電視上的「自願認罪」幾乎全部是經過長期非法羈押、酷刑與藥物折磨的劇本表演。我們紀念江天勇與所有 709 維權律師——他們以肉身代價，告訴世人何謂真正的法律人。

Jiang Tianyong is one of the most active human rights lawyers in China since the 2000s, having represented Falun Gong practitioners, Tibetans, petitioners, HIV-positive plaintiffs, and underground church members. After the 709 Crackdown he continued to support the families of detained colleagues, and was finally seized in late 2016. His case lays bare the realities of televised "confessions" and systematic torture in China.

From Village Teacher to Rights Lawyer

Born in Henan in 1971, Jiang first taught history in a rural middle school. After qualifying as a lawyer in 2003, he plunged into public-interest litigation. With Xu Zhiyong and Teng Biao he co-founded the Open Constitution Initiative (Gongmeng) and became a core figure of China's first generation of rights lawyers. He was repeatedly disbarred, beaten, and briefly detained.

Disappearance After Helping 709 Families

After July 2015, Jiang became one of the main legal advisers to families of the detained 709 lawyers, supporting figures such as Li Wenzu (wife of Wang Quanzhang) and Chen Guiqiu (wife of Xie Yang). On November 21, 2016, he disappeared after meeting Xie Yang's wife in Changsha, Hunan. Authorities initially denied involvement and only acknowledged his detention 36 days later.

"Residential Surveillance" and Torture Allegations

During his Residential Surveillance at a Designated Location, Jiang was reportedly deprived of sleep, forced to take unknown medication, and subjected to extended interrogations in stress positions. In a May 2017 court appearance broadcast on CCTV, he "confessed" to all charges and repudiated his prior advocacy. His wife Jin Bianling stated this was utterly inconsistent with his pre-detention character and convictions. Many lawyers suspect he suffered severe psychological and pharmacological abuse.

Sentence and "Soft Release"

On November 21, 2017, the Changsha Intermediate People's Court sentenced Jiang to two years for "inciting subversion of state power." After completing his sentence on February 28, 2019, he was sent back to his hometown in Henan, where he remains under de facto house arrest, his movements tightly monitored, unable to resume legal practice.

How Televised Confessions Are Manufactured

Jiang's case shows that televised confession is often not a judicial act but a political communication project. First, secret detention cuts off lawyers and family. Then torture, medication, or psychological pressure breaks the detainee's will. Finally, state media edits the result into images of "voluntary remorse." These images are not evidence; they are tools for intimidating other legal professionals.

Conclusion: Confessions Are Not the Truth

Jiang's case exposes a stark reality: nearly every "voluntary confession" broadcast on Chinese state TV is the product of prolonged extralegal detention, torture, and drugging. We remember Jiang Tianyong and all 709 lawyers—their bodies have shown the world what it truly means to be a person of the law.
