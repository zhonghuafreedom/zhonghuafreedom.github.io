豐縣「鐵鏈女」事件：被遺忘的拐賣婦女 The "Chained Woman" of Feng County: Trafficked Women China Forgot

2022年1月28日，一段拍攝於江蘇徐州豐縣董集村的影片在中國網路引爆全國憤怒：一名中年婦女僅穿單衣，脖頸被鐵鏈鎖在破舊小屋的牆上，神情呆滯，正寒冬中發抖。這名後被官方稱為「楊某俠」的女子，在此處已生育八個孩子。

八個孩子背後的真相

影片中的男子董志民先因「八孩父親」被當地宣傳為脫貧典型，事件曝光後輿論逆轉。網民追問：一個四十出頭的農村男子，為何能合法生育八胎？她的真實姓名與來歷為何？她為什麼被鎖鏈？

四份矛盾的官方通報

從1月28日至2月23日，地方政府接連發布四份通報，每一份都推翻前一份。先稱「不存在拐賣」，再稱「她叫楊某俠」，再稱她實為來自雲南福貢縣的「小花梅」，曾被多次轉賣。每次通報都激起更大民憤，「我們不需要新的小花梅」成為熱搜口號。

背後的拐賣網路

該事件揭開了中國農村長期存在的婦女拐賣黑幕。據不完全統計，1990年代至今，至少有數十萬名雲南、貴州、四川的少數民族婦女與精神障礙者被販運至華北、華東農村。地方政府常以「維穩」與「人口任務」之名選擇性失明。

調查記者與公民被打壓

多名前往豐縣調查的公民——「烏衣」、「權平」等——在到達當天即被警方帶走、行政拘留。多名律師為小花梅提出申請會見、要求 DNA 比對被拒。微博上幾乎所有相關話題都被限流或刪除，「鐵鏈女」一詞至今仍是敏感詞。

結語：一條鐵鏈，鎖住的不只是一個人

小花梅的故事不是孤例，而是被結構性遺忘的中國農村女性的縮影。當一個女子被鎖在鐵鏈下生育八個孩子卻無人察覺、無人解救，這不僅是個人的悲劇，更是制度與社會集體的罪。我們紀念她，是為了讓所有「被消失」的女性重新擁有名字。

On January 28, 2022, a short video filmed in Dongji Village, Feng County, Xuzhou, Jiangsu Province sparked nationwide outrage. A middle-aged woman, dressed in a single thin layer in mid-winter, was shown chained by the neck to the wall of a dilapidated shed, trembling and dazed. Officials later referred to her as "Yang Mouxia." She had given birth to eight children at this site.

The Truth Behind the Eight Children

The man, Dong Zhimin, had previously been promoted by local propaganda as a "father of eight" who overcame poverty. Once the video surfaced, public opinion turned. Netizens demanded answers: how could a rural man in his early forties legally have eight children? What was her real name? Why was she in chains?

Four Contradictory Official Statements

Between January 28 and February 23, the local government issued four statements, each contradicting the previous one. First, "no trafficking occurred." Then, "her name is Yang Mouxia." Finally, that she was actually "Xiaohuamei" from Fugong County, Yunnan, having been resold multiple times. Each statement provoked greater public fury. "We do not need another Xiaohuamei" became a viral slogan.

The Trafficking Network Behind Her

The case exposed a long-standing dark trade in China's countryside. Conservative estimates indicate that since the 1990s, hundreds of thousands of women and persons with mental disabilities—mostly from ethnic minorities in Yunnan, Guizhou, and Sichuan—have been trafficked to rural North and East China. Local governments have routinely turned a blind eye in the name of "stability" and "population quotas."

Citizen Investigators Suppressed

Several citizen investigators who travelled to Feng County—"Wuyi," "Quanping," and others—were detained by police on the day they arrived. Lawyers who requested meetings with Xiaohuamei or demanded DNA verification were refused. Almost all related Weibo topics were throttled or deleted, and "chained woman" remains a censored term to this day.

Conclusion: A Chain That Bound More Than One Person

Xiaohuamei's story is not an isolated case but a microcosm of the structural erasure of rural Chinese women. When a woman can be chained and forced to bear eight children unnoticed and unrescued, it is not merely a personal tragedy—it is a collective crime of system and society. We remember her so that every "disappeared" woman may once again have a name.
