銅鑼灣書店事件：跨境綁架的開端 Causeway Bay Books: The Opening Act of Cross-Border Abduction

銅鑼灣書店是香港一間以出售批評中共領導人書籍聞名的小書店。2015年10月至12月，書店股東與員工林榮基、桂民海、呂波、張志平、李波五人，竟先後在香港、深圳、泰國「人間蒸發」，揭開「一國兩制」之下、北京跨境綁架的序幕。

五人連環失蹤

2015年10月14日，書店股東桂民海在泰國芭達雅住所被「便衣男子」帶走；隨後呂波、張志平在深圳失聯；總經理林榮基於 10 月 24 日由深圳入境後失蹤；最令人震驚的是，李波於 12 月 30 日於香港銅鑼灣書店倉庫被帶走，他甚至沒有帶走自己的回港證——這意味著有人在香港境內將他帶過了深圳河。

電視認罪秀

2016年1月17日，桂民海在中國央視出現「自首」，聲淚俱下承認 2003 年寧波一起酒駕肇事案，並表示「自願回國接受法律制裁」。2 月 28 日，李波在央視為自己「以非正常方式回到內地」道歉，宣稱「我不需要香港特區政府幫助」。觀察人士普遍認為這是被迫表演。

林榮基的證詞

2016年6月16日，林榮基獲准短暫返港，本應回深圳「交付硬碟」。他卻在香港召開記者會，揭露被秘密拘押 8 個月、被強迫認罪、被要求交出讀者名單的全部過程。他的證詞震動國際，使「一國兩制」的虛偽暴露無遺。林榮基隨後流亡台灣，2020年於台北重開「銅鑼灣書店」。

桂民海的延續迫害

桂民海持有瑞典國籍，2017年「獲釋」後仍被軟禁。2018年1月，他在從寧波赴北京瑞典大使館的列車上當著瑞典外交官面被便衣特工再次帶走。2020年2月被以「為境外非法提供情報罪」判處有期徒刑10年，剝奪政治權利5年。

結語：當邊界不再保護人

銅鑼灣書店事件證明了一件事：自 2015 年起，香港不再是安全之地，外國護照不再是護身符。它預示了五年後港版國安法的全面降臨，更敲響了所有出版批評書籍者的警鐘。

Causeway Bay Books was a small Hong Kong bookstore famous for selling titles critical of Chinese leaders. Between October and December 2015, five of its shareholders and staff—Lam Wing-kee, Gui Minhai, Lui Bo, Cheung Chi-ping, and Lee Bo—vanished from Hong Kong, Shenzhen, and Thailand, opening the era of Beijing's cross-border abductions under "One Country, Two Systems."

Five Sequential Disappearances

On October 14, 2015, shareholder Gui Minhai was taken by "men in plain clothes" from his Pattaya residence in Thailand. Lui Bo and Cheung Chi-ping disappeared in Shenzhen shortly after. Manager Lam Wing-kee vanished after entering Shenzhen on October 24. Most shockingly, Lee Bo was taken from the bookstore's warehouse in Causeway Bay on December 30—without his Home Return Permit, meaning someone had transported him out of Hong Kong by extralegal means.

Televised Forced Confessions

On January 17, 2016, Gui Minhai appeared on China's CCTV "confessing" tearfully to a 2003 drunk-driving case in Ningbo and declaring he had "voluntarily returned to face the law." On February 28, Lee Bo appeared on CCTV apologizing for "returning to the mainland through irregular means" and refusing assistance from the Hong Kong government. Observers widely regard these as scripted, coerced performances.

Lam Wing-kee's Testimony

On June 16, 2016, Lam was permitted a short return trip to Hong Kong supposedly to retrieve a hard drive. Instead, he held a press conference revealing he had been secretly detained for eight months, forced to confess, and pressured to hand over a list of customers. His testimony shocked the world and exposed the hollowness of "One Country, Two Systems." He later fled to Taiwan and reopened "Causeway Bay Books" in Taipei in 2020.

Continued Persecution of Gui Minhai

Gui, a Swedish citizen, was "released" in 2017 but kept under restriction. In January 2018, he was seized again from a train to Beijing in front of Swedish diplomats. In February 2020 he was sentenced to 10 years in prison for "illegally providing intelligence to foreign entities," plus five years' deprivation of political rights.

Conclusion: When Borders Stop Protecting People

The Causeway Bay Books case proved one thing: from 2015 onward, Hong Kong was no longer safe and a foreign passport was no longer a shield. It foreshadowed the full arrival of the National Security Law five years later—and serves as a warning to anyone publishing critical books anywhere.
